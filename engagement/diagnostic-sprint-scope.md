## What this engagement is — and what it is not

This is a **diagnostic sprint**, not a consulting retainer.

A retainer sells time. A diagnostic sprint sells a specific answer to a specific question. The question here is: *Is the Aircom CES hypothesis of 42/100 correct, partially correct, or wrong — and what does the confirmed score mean for Aircom's governance posture and revenue exposure?*

The sprint ends when that question is answered. The deliverable is a confirmed CES scorecard, a revenue exposure quantification based on internal data rather than public inference, and a governance remediation roadmap scoped to whatever gaps the confirmed score reveals.

There is no open-ended scope. There is no scope creep by design. Every week has a defined output. The engagement closes when the deliverable is complete — typically in three weeks, four if access coordination requires it.

## The two-outcome model

This sprint is structured so that both possible outcomes produce something Aircom did not have before it started.

### Outcome A — Hypothesis confirmed (CES holds at or below 50)

Aircom receives:

- A confirmed CES scorecard replacing the public-data hypothesis with internal architecture data
- A quantified Silent Distortion Window based on actual system behavior, not estimated parameters
- A revenue exposure model built on Aircom's confirmed quote volume, deal size, and close rate
- A governance remediation roadmap identifying the highest-priority missing layers in priority sequence
- A board-ready risk summary suitable for investor reporting, audit committee review, or vendor procurement conversations

The roadmap identifies the path from confirmed score to above 80 (self-correcting threshold), with estimated implementation effort and cost for each layer. A follow-on remediation engagement is available but not assumed.

### Outcome B — Hypothesis partially or fully disproved (CES rises above 60)

Aircom receives:

- A confirmed CES scorecard documenting which governance layers are in place and functioning
- A competitive differentiation artifact — documented AI governance architecture benchmarked against Amazon and Airbus — suitable for board reporting, investor due diligence, and enterprise customer procurement responses
- A gap analysis identifying any remaining watch-zone items with recommended monitoring cadence
- A public summary (optional, Aircom-approved) that can be referenced in sales, investor, and regulatory contexts as evidence of governance maturity

As AI governance becomes a procurement criterion — particularly under the Colorado AI Act (effective June 2026) and California ADMT requirements (effective January 2027) — a documented CES above 60 is a tangible competitive asset. This sprint produces that documentation whether or not the hypothesis holds.

## Week-by-week scope

### Week 1 — Architecture Intake

**Objective:** Understand how Aircom's AI quoting system was built, how its decision criteria were encoded, and what mechanisms currently exist for criteria review.

**Sessions:** Kickoff call (90 min, CTO) + one working session (60 min, VP Engineering)

**Work product:**
- Criteria encoding map — who defined the original decision rules, when, and through what process
- Current-state governance inventory — what review processes exist today, in any form
- Data access agreement — confirmation of which internal data sets are available for Weeks 2 and 3

**Output document:** Architecture Intake Summary (internal working document, not part of final deliverable)

---

### Week 2 — Benchmark Mapping

**Objective:** Score each of the five CES layers against confirmed internal architecture data and update the hypothesis scores with confirmed figures.

**Sessions:** Weekly check-in (30 min)

**Work product:**
- Five-layer CES scoring against confirmed architecture — hypothesis scores replaced with confirmed scores where evidence is available
- Gap identification — confirmed gaps versus hypothesis gaps; any layers where evidence is ambiguous flagged for Week 3 follow-up
- Benchmark comparison updated — Amazon and Airbus benchmarks held constant; Aircom column updated from hypothesis to confirmed

**Output document:** Confirmed CES Scorecard Draft (shared with CTO for accuracy review before Week 3)

---

### Week 3 — Silent Distortion Audit

**Objective:** Quantify the actual Silent Distortion Window from internal quote outcome data and build the revenue exposure model on confirmed operating parameters.

**Sessions:** Working session (60 min, CTO or VP Engineering with quote outcome data access)

**Work product:**
- Silent Distortion Window quantification — actual window duration from internal divergence data, replacing the 30–90 day hypothesis estimate
- Revenue exposure model — built on confirmed quote volume, deal size, close rate, and confirmed misprice rate from internal data
- Remediation priority stack — if gaps are confirmed, layers are ranked by revenue impact per dollar of remediation effort

**Output document:** Silent Distortion Audit Working File (internal; inputs flow directly into final deliverable)

> **Note:** If Week 2 confirms the hypothesis is largely disproved (CES above 70), Week 3 pivots from audit to validation documentation — building the governance artifact for Outcome B rather than the remediation roadmap for Outcome A. Scope and timeline remain unchanged.

---

### Week 4 — Deliverable and Readout

**Objective:** Produce the final confirmed CES scorecard, revenue exposure quantification, and governance roadmap. Present findings in a 60-minute readout session.

**Sessions:** Final readout (60 min, CTO + any additional stakeholders Aircom chooses to include)

**Final deliverable package:**

| Document | Format | Audience |
|---|---|---|
| Confirmed CES Scorecard | PDF + editable source | CTO, board, investors |
| Revenue Exposure Model | PDF + Excel | CFO, board |
| Governance Remediation Roadmap (if applicable) | PDF | CTO, CEO |
| Competitive Governance Validation (if applicable) | PDF | CEO, sales, investors |
| Executive Summary | One-page PDF | CEO, board |

All documents are delivered in Aircom-brandable formats. Framework attribution (McDonald, E.M., 2026) is noted in methodology footnotes; it does not appear in executive-facing pages.

## Pricing rationale

The $8,500 to $12,000 price range reflects two variables resolved at kickoff: access complexity and data availability.

**$8,500** applies when:
- CTO is the single point of contact with direct data access
- Quote outcome data is queryable without significant engineering lift
- No additional stakeholder coordination is required

**$12,000** applies when:
- Multiple stakeholders require coordination across departments
- Data access requires engineering support to extract or format
- Week 3 requires an additional working session to resolve data access gaps

The final price is confirmed in the engagement agreement signed before kickoff. There are no change orders, no hourly overages, and no scope additions without a separate written agreement.

### Sprint ROI at hypothesis parameters

At conservative Aircom operating assumptions (500 monthly quotes, $8,500 average deal size, 28% close rate), the estimated 90-day Silent Distortion Window exposure is approximately $57,000. A $10,000 sprint that confirms this exposure returns approximately 5–6x on the confirmation alone — before any remediation savings are counted.

If the hypothesis is wrong and the sprint produces a CES above 60, the governance validation artifact has measurable value in enterprise sales cycles, investor due diligence, and regulatory compliance contexts. The Colorado AI Act (effective June 2026) and California ADMT requirements (effective January 2027) both create contexts where documented AI governance architecture commands a procurement premium.

Either way, the sprint produces more value than it costs.

## What the sprint does not include

To be explicit about scope boundaries:

- **No remediation implementation.** The sprint diagnoses and documents. It does not build, configure, or deploy governance architecture. A separate remediation engagement is available if the confirmed score warrants it.
- **No ongoing monitoring.** The sprint produces a point-in-time assessment. Ongoing CES monitoring is available as a separate retainer if desired.
- **No vendor recommendations.** The sprint identifies governance gaps; it does not recommend specific technology vendors to fill them. Vendor-neutral implementation guidance is available in a follow-on engagement.
- **No public disclosure without approval.** Nothing from the sprint is published, referenced publicly, or used in any Behavioral Intelligence Research or Epoch Frameworks LLC marketing materials without explicit written approval from Aircom.

## The follow-on engagement (for reference only)

If the diagnostic sprint confirms the CES hypothesis and Aircom chooses to pursue remediation, a follow-on governance remediation engagement is available. It is scoped separately after the sprint closes, priced based on confirmed gap severity, and typically runs 60 to 90 days.

The remediation engagement targets a confirmed CES above 80 — the self-correcting threshold — and produces implemented governance architecture across the three highest-priority missing layers. Estimated price range based on typical logistics AI governance remediations: $15,000 to $22,000 per month for the engagement duration.

This is provided for planning context only. No assumption of follow-on work is embedded in the diagnostic sprint agreement.

## How to engage

The diagnostic sprint begins with a 20-minute scoping call to confirm access parameters and finalize pricing within the stated range. Following that call, Behavioral Intelligence Research provides:

1. A mutual NDA (Epoch Frameworks LLC template, or Aircom template accepted)
2. A one-page engagement agreement with fixed price, scope, and deliverable list

No procurement process, no RFP, no legal complexity beyond the NDA and the one-page agreement. The sprint is designed to start within five business days of a signed agreement.
